# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Founders, directors, and operations leads of small-to-mid businesses in Sri Lanka (Colombo-centric market) who need professional business services — company secretarial, accounting, tax advisory, legal, HR, and marketing — but don't want to juggle six separate firms. They arrive either from local/organic search ("company secretarial Colombo", "tax advisory Sri Lanka") or from referral, usually while actively evaluating a provider for a real, often time-sensitive obligation (incorporation, compliance filing, hiring). Their context is high-stakes and low-patience: they're comparing options and deciding whom to trust with sensitive, consequential work.

The job to be done: quickly understand what OneDesk covers, believe it's competent and credible, and take one clear next step — reach out or request a consultation.

## Product Purpose

OneDesk is an all-in-one business-services provider that consolidates six disciplines (secretarial, accounting, tax, legal, HR, marketing) under one roof. The site's job is to make that "one desk instead of six firms" proposition instantly legible and credible, and to convert that credibility into qualified inquiries.

Success is **lead-generation led**: the site must build genuine brand credibility, but its outcome is measured in qualified contact submissions and consultation requests. Brand impression is the means; the lead is the end.

## Brand Personality

**Premium · confident · expert.** OneDesk speaks with the calm authority of a specialist who has handled this many times before — reassuring rather than salesy, precise rather than flashy. Voice is clear and plain-spoken (it demystifies rather than gatekeeps), but the finish is polished and high-end. The visitor should feel they've found the serious, obvious choice — the firm that makes complex obligations feel handled.

Emotional goal: **relief and confidence.** "These people know what they're doing, and I can hand this to them."

## Anti-references

- **Generic corporate / template.** No stock-photo handshakes, no blue-gradient SaaS template, no faceless big-firm blandness. OneDesk has a real identity (the six-pillar color system, the rainbow header signature) — never let it read as a bought theme.
- **Cheap / discount services.** No loud pricing banners, no clutter, no budget-tier freelancer-marketplace energy. Premium services can't look like a coupon.
- **Startup / techy SaaS.** No dev-tool dark mode, no neon gradients, no product-feature jargon. This is professional services delivered by people, not software.
- **Stuffy / old-fashioned legal.** No dusty serif law-firm look, no intimidating dense text walls, no cold formality. Authority without stiffness.

## Design Principles

- **Credibility is the product.** Every choice either earns trust or spends it. Real names, real faces, real clients, and precise copy beat decorative flourish — for a services firm, proof of competence is the strongest aesthetic.
- **One desk, one system.** The six-pillar color system is the brand's central metaphor made visual: six disciplines, one coherent surface. Preserve and use it systematically; it's what makes OneDesk un-templated.
- **Clear over clever.** Demystify. A visitor should understand what's covered and what to do next without decoding jargon or hunting for the CTA. Plain language is a premium signal here, not a downgrade.
- **Polished, never loud.** Confidence is quiet. Restraint in motion and color, precision in typography and spacing — the finish signals quality; shouting signals discount.
- **Every path leads to contact.** Lead-gen led means the next step is always in reach. Sections build belief, then hand the visitor a low-friction way to reach out.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**. Known priorities (from the existing design audit): a global `:focus-visible` outline for keyboard users, a body/label contrast pass (especially small overline text on light pastel tints), comfortable tap-target sizing, and keeping the closed mobile menu out of the tab order. `prefers-reduced-motion` is already respected globally — keep every new animation gated behind a reduced-motion alternative.
